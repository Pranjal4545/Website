Information Technology

Information Technology (IT) is the use of computers and software to manage information.

What is Computer Ethics?

Computer ethics refers to the set of moral principles that govern the usage of computers and information systems.

Commandments of Computer Ethics:

  • Do not use a computer to harm other people.
  • Do not interfere with other people’s computer work.
  • Do not snoop around in other people’s computer files.
  • Do not use a computer to steal.
  • Do not use a computer to bear false witness.
  • Do not copy or use proprietary software for which you have not paid.
  • Do not use other people’s computer resources without authorization.
  • Think about the social consequences of the program you are writing.
  • Use a computer in ways that ensure consideration and respect for others.

Role of Computer Ethics

Computer ethics plays a crucial role in forming a secure and reliable digital society by guiding users on responsible behavior online. It helps reduce cybercrimes, ensures respect for privacy and intellectual property, promotes fairness, and builds trust among digital users.

Basic Principles of Information Security

  • Confidentiality: Protecting information from unauthorized access.
  • Integrity: Ensuring data remains accurate and unaltered.
  • Availability: Ensuring information is available when needed.
  • Authentication: Verifying the identity of users and systems.
  • Non-repudiation: Ensuring that actions cannot be denied.

Cyber Law

Cyber law governs digital activities and ensures legal recognition of electronic records, preventing cybercrimes, and safeguarding rights in the digital world.

Major Fields of Cyber Law:

  • Cybercrime control
  • Protection of intellectual property rights
  • Data protection and privacy laws
  • Electronic contracts and digital signatures
  • E-commerce and online transaction regulations

Common Questions and Answers

1. How do you know if your computer has been a victim of malware?

  • Slow performance
  • Frequent crashes
  • Unusual pop-ups
  • Programs opening automatically

2. How can you protect your system from cybercrime?

  • Use strong passwords
  • Install antivirus software
  • Enable firewalls
  • Be cautious with emails and links

3. What are the advantages and disadvantages of digital signatures?

Advantages Disadvantages
Provides authenticity and integrity of documents Requires digital certificates
Legally recognized Can be compromised if the private key is stolen
Saves time and cost Acceptance issues in some regions

4. Explain various areas covered in cyber law.

  • Protection against cybercrimes
  • Intellectual property rights in cyberspace
  • Data protection and privacy
  • Regulation of electronic signatures

5. Discuss cyber law in Nepal.

Cyber law in Nepal is governed by the Electronic Transaction Act (ETA) 2063, which includes provisions for:

  • Legal recognition of electronic transactions
  • Punishments for cybercrimes
  • Protection of intellectual property rights

6. What are the major objectives of the ICT Policy 2072?

  • Promote ICT for national development
  • Expand ICT infrastructure to rural areas
  • Encourage e-governance and digital services
  • Develop skilled human resources in ICT
  • Increase public access to ICT services
